It was one of these sunken cost fallacies: I already had the [[Pentium G2030]] with 4GB RAM (2x 2GB) from the old mining project with the RX470. But the Pentium is really slow. I had a newer RGB case, the GPU was there, and the PSU as well - just mainboard and CPU! The Xeon would be my third Xeon computer (after the [[HP Z600 Workstation]] and [[E5-2696 v3 with 128 GB RAM]]).
## Xeon or i5?
For the Socket 1150 it was actually **cheaper** than a similar i5-4590 (350k vs. 460k VND) for the same 4C/4T and 3.3-3.7 GHz. As I found out, it also has the HD 4600 GPU integrated! A little unusual for a Xeon, but here we are!
## RAM upgrade to 10 GB
For just 200k you get a 8GB DDR3 1600 memory DIMM. And then it is useable with Windows 10. Two years later the price has increased to 490k, that's early 2026!
## CPU price history
The Xeon [E3-1226 v3](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/products/sku/80917/intel-xeon-processor-e31226-v3-8m-cache-3-30-ghz/specifications.html) used to be more expensive: 213 USD while the [i5-4590](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/products/sku/80815/intel-core-i54590-processor-6m-cache-up-to-3-70-ghz/specifications.html) was just 192 USD. 10 years later the service updates have ended in 2021, and it is now 13 USD or 350k VND versus 18 USD or 460k VND.
